About & Approach

Background

My background spans UX research, UX/UI design, AI evaluation, publishing, multilingual communication, and digital humanities. I'm currently completing an MSc in Digital Humanities at University College London (UCL) — combining advanced research methods, human-centred design, NLP, data visualisation, and responsible AI.

Educated in Comparative Literature (MA & BA, Jadavpur University), I bring a humanistic lens to design problems — attending closely to language, context, and the people behind the data.

My Approach

I trained as a comparatist before I trained as a designer. Reading across languages and cultures taught me to notice what gets lost in translation — and to ask who a system was built for, and who it quietly leaves out.

Where does a product break down for someone it wasn't designed for? That's the question I bring to every brief.

Good design, to me, is usable, accessible, and sustainable by default — not bolted on as an afterthought, but treated as the starting constraint. My MSc in Digital Humanities deepens that commitment: bridging research methods, ethical AI, and human-centred practice.

I work across research, interface design, and strategy — wherever the problem sits.
